Dyson SwarmFAR

Millions of orbital solar collectors harvesting the sun's full 3.8×10²⁶W output — compared to ~1.7×10¹⁷W reaching Earth. The defining marker of a Kardashev Type II civilisation. Requires post-scarcity coordination, self-replicating manufacturing on Mercury, and millennia-scale construction. No current program — pure civilisational engineering theory.

NO CURRENT PROGRAM — CONCEPT: FREEMAN DYSON (1960) · REQUIRES TYPE II CIVILISATION COORDINATION
Orbital Ring MegastructureFAR

A rigid ring at orbital altitude (~10,000 km), rotating faster than orbital velocity so magnetic levitation cancels gravitational pull. Surface-connected habitats hang below via tethers; launch ramps embedded in the ring allow mass-to-orbit delivery at a fraction of rocket cost. The theoretically cheapest possible mass-to-orbit access — $1/kg vs $2,000+/kg today. Concept: Paul Birch (1982). No current program.

NO CURRENT PROGRAM — CONCEPT: PAUL BIRCH (1982) · ISAACSON / SKYLON TRAJECTORY
Stellar LiftingFAR

Extracting matter directly from stars using magnetic plasma jets — inducing mass ejections and capturing ejected plasma. Would extend stellar lifetimes by removing the fuel that accelerates stellar death, and provide raw fusion fuel for an interstellar civilisation. Proposed mechanism: intense magnetic fields threading through the stellar atmosphere. Pure astroengineering theory — no current program.

Matrioshka Brain — Stellar-Scale ComputingFAR

A Matrioshka Brain is a hypothetical megastructure of concentric Dyson shells wrapped around a star, each layer capturing the waste heat of the layer inside it, converting stellar output into computation. A solar-powered Matrioshka Brain could sustain 10²³ to 10²⁶ bits per second of computation — enough to simulate every mind in human history simultaneously, millions of times over. The concept, coined by Robert Bradbury (2001), represents the theoretical computational ceiling of a Type II civilisation. Every layer runs at progressively lower temperatures, extracting maximum work from photons.

THEORETICAL PHYSICS — NO CURRENT DEVELOPERS. ADJACENT: DYSON SWARM PROJECTS, BREAKTHROUGH INITIATIVES
Whole Brain Emulation & Substrate-Independent MindsFAR

Whole Brain Emulation (WBE) requires scanning a brain at sufficient resolution to capture all synaptic connections (~100 trillion), then running the resulting connectome as software. The Human Connectome Project mapped small portions of mouse cortex — 1mm³ contained 100 trillion synaptic connections, 1.4 petabytes of data. Full human WBE may require scanning at nanometre resolution (electron microscopy), currently a 300-year computational task at current speeds. Substrate-independent minds could run faster, fork, merge, and survive indefinitely — fundamentally changing the meaning of identity and death.

Interstellar Propulsion — Starshot, Nuclear Pulse & AntimatterFAR

Three interstellar propulsion concepts have serious scientific backing. Breakthrough Starshot (Yuri Milner / Stephen Hawking): gram-scale light sails accelerated to 20% light-speed by Earth-based laser arrays — reaching Alpha Centauri in 20 years. Nuclear Pulse Propulsion (Project Orion revival): nuclear explosions behind a pusher plate could propel a crewed ship to 10% light-speed. Antimatter propulsion: annihilating matter and antimatter releases 100× more energy than fusion per kg — but producing 1g of antimatter currently costs $25 quadrillion. All three face decade-scale R&D requirements before any interstellar demonstration mission.

Artificial General Superintelligence & Post-Human CivilisationSPECULATIVE

AGI systems surpassing human cognitive ability across all domains will emerge somewhere in the 20–50 year window under most serious forecasts. The transition from narrow AI to AGI will likely be rapid once achieved — possibly months, not decades. Post-AGI civilisation faces the question of human purpose, governance of minds more capable than their creators, and the redefinition of creativity, labour, and consciousness. The transition is not the end of humanity's story — it may be the beginning of its most interesting chapter.

Molecular Nanotechnology — Drexlerian AssemblersSPECULATIVE

K. Eric Drexler's 1986 "Engines of Creation" proposed molecular assemblers — devices capable of building any physically possible structure atom by atom. Modern molecular machines (Nobel Prize 2016) demonstrate controllable molecular motors, switches, and rotors. Full programmable molecular manufacturing remains decades away but is not physically prohibited — it requires solving protein folding for arbitrary structures, error correction at the atomic level, and feedstock delivery at nanoscale. If achieved, it eliminates scarcity of physical objects entirely.

CALTECH NANOTECHNOLOGY · ETH ZURICH · ZYVEX LABS · FORESIGHT INSTITUTE · NANOTECH INSTITUTE (UT DALLAS)
Terraforming — Planetary-Scale EngineeringSPECULATIVE

Mars terraforming requires raising surface pressure 100× and temperature 60°C, establishing a water cycle, and generating a magnetosphere — a 500–1000 year project under optimistic timelines. Proposals include: orbital mirror arrays to warm the poles (releasing CO₂), engineered methanogen microbes for greenhouse gas production, and ferromagnetic asteroid impacts to seed a planetary magnetic field. Venus terraforming (removing CO₂, cooling) is arguably harder but closer to habitable parameters if successful. Both are speculative engineering at civilisation scale.

Dyson Sphere / Dyson Swarm — Total Stellar Energy CaptureCIVILISATION-SCALE

A Dyson Sphere (solid shell) or Dyson Swarm (millions of orbiting collectors) captures 100% of a star's energy output — 3.8 × 10²⁶ watts for our Sun. This represents the defining achievement of a Type II civilisation on the Kardashev scale. A Dyson Swarm is the realistic first step: launching solar panels into orbit progressively over centuries until stellar energy is fully captured. Astronomers search for Dyson spheres around other stars as a technosignature of advanced civilisations — the Tabby's Star anomaly sparked significant investigation.

THEORETICAL PHYSICS / KARDASHEV CIVILISATION THEORY — ADJACENT: BREAKTHROUGH STARSHOT · SETI INSTITUTE
Galactic-Scale Civilisation — Intergalactic ColonisationCIVILISATION-SCALE

A civilisation expanding at even 1% the speed of light could colonise the Milky Way's 100 billion star systems within 10 million years — a cosmic eyeblink. Von Neumann probes (self-replicating spacecraft) could seed millions of star systems simultaneously. The Fermi Paradox — why have we detected no evidence of such civilisations — remains the deepest unsolved question in science. Possible answers: civilisations self-destruct before reaching galactic scale; they exist but are undetectable; we are early; or the universe is structured to prevent galaxy-spanning civilisations.
