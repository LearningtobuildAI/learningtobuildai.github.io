Soft Robotics — Biologically-Inspired MachinesNEAR

Soft robotics replaces rigid actuators with flexible, compliant materials — silicone, shape-memory polymers, hydrogels, and pneumatic networks — enabling robots that can safely touch human bodies, squeeze through tight spaces, and handle fragile objects. Harvard SEAS soft robots navigate obstacle courses, open valves, and swim in water. Medical applications include cardiac sleeves that assist failing hearts, soft endoscopes for minimally invasive surgery, and exosuits for stroke rehabilitation. Agricultural grippers sort fruit without bruising. The field's core challenge is replacing stiff electronics with equally flexible sensing and computation — stretchable circuit boards using liquid metal conductors are the current frontier.
