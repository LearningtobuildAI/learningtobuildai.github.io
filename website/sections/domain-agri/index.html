Precision Fermentation & Alternative ProteinsNOW

Precision fermentation uses engineered microbes to produce identical animal proteins — dairy whey (Perfect Day), egg proteins (Every Company), collagen (Geltor) — without any animals. Economics crossed parity with conventional dairy whey in 2024. At scale: 99% less land, 90% less water, identical molecular products. The global alternative protein market reached $8B in 2025, with precision fermentation the fastest-growing segment at 40% CAGR.

Vertical Farming at Commercial ScaleNEAR

AI-controlled indoor farms growing 365 days/year with 95% less water, zero pesticides, and 10–100× yield per m² versus field farming. The expansion from leafy greens to calorie crops (wheat, rice, soybeans) is the key unlock — requiring 30–40% reductions in LED energy costs currently underway. Bowery Farming, Plenty (SoftBank), and AeroFarms lead US deployment; Nordic Harvest and IGS lead Europe.

Lab-Grown Meat at Price ParityFAR

Cultivated meat (real animal muscle grown from cells, no slaughter) approved in Singapore and USA, but currently costs $17–30/kg — 3–5× conventional beef. Reaching price parity requires 100× cost reduction in growth media (currently 70–80% of production cost). Upside Foods, Eat Just, and Mosa Meat project price parity by 2030–2035. At parity, cultivated meat eliminates 15% of global greenhouse gas emissions and 80% of antibiotic usage in one transition.
