Enhanced Geothermal SystemsNEAR

Enhanced geothermal systems (EGS) drill deep into hot dry rock, fracture it hydraulically, and circulate water to extract heat independent of natural hydrothermal resources. Fervo Energy in Utah and Nevada is producing commercial geothermal power from EGS wells. The US DOE estimates the continental US contains enough EGS resource to power the country 100 times over. Unlike solar and wind, geothermal is 24/7 baseload. Drilling cost reduction — driven by oil-and-gas techniques and AI-optimised drill path planning — is the key enabling technology.

FERVO ENERGY · ALTAROCK · QUAISE · BEDROCK ENERGY
Green Hydrogen EconomyNEAR

Electrolysis-produced green hydrogen using surplus renewable electricity is scaling. Electrolyser costs have fallen 60% since 2020. Green hydrogen is being piloted for steel production (replacing coking coal), long-haul shipping fuel, and seasonal energy storage. Germany H2Global, Australia H2 Hub, and NEOM NEOM Green Hydrogen Company are building gigawatt-scale production. The challenge is transport and storage: hydrogen is the smallest molecule and leaks through most containment materials. Green ammonia as a hydrogen carrier is the leading solution for intercontinental trade.

H2GLOBAL · ALKALINE ELECTROLYSERS · NEOM GREEN HYDROGEN · THYSSENKRUPP NUCERA · PLUG POWER
Active Space Debris Removal & Orbital SustainabilityNEAR

670,000+ debris fragments larger than 1cm orbit Earth, each capable of catastrophic spacecraft damage at 28,000 km/h. The Kessler Syndrome — a cascade of collisions rendering orbits unusable — is no longer theoretical: the 2021 Russian ASAT test created 1,500+ trackable fragments. ClearSpace-1 (ESA, 2026 launch) will capture and deorbit a Vespa upper stage — the first active debris removal mission. Astroscale's ELSA-d proved magnetic capture of tumbling debris. Northrop Grumman's MEV-1 extended a commercial satellite's life by 5 years via docking. Space sustainability is the prerequisite for the entire space economy.
