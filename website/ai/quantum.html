Quantum Computing

Operational view of qubits, fault tolerance, and hardware race conditions.

Qubits

Qubits encode superposition states for parallel probability amplitudes, enabling algorithms that can outperform classical workflows for selected problem classes.

Error Correction

Fault-tolerant quantum systems require logical qubits built from many physical qubits, using repeated syndrome extraction and stabilizer decoding to suppress noise.

Quantum Hardware

Current stacks include superconducting, trapped-ion, neutral atom, and photonic approaches, each with trade-offs across coherence time, gate fidelity, and scaling.
